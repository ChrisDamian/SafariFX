# CENTRAL BANK OF KENYA

## MONETARY POLICY COMMITTEE (MPC) COMMUNIQUÉ

**Meeting No. 143 — June 4, 2026**

---

### 1. MPC Decision Summary

The Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) met on June 4, 2026, to review the prevailing macroeconomic conditions, the outlook for inflation, and the stability of the financial system. After comprehensive deliberation, the Committee decided to:

> **Retain the Central Bank Rate (CBR) at 10.00 percent.**

The Committee noted that overall inflation remained within the target range, anchored at 5.8 percent in May 2026, and that the economy continues to operate close to its potential. While global commodity prices have exhibited modest upward pressure, domestic demand-driven inflation risks remain contained.

The Committee will continue to closely monitor developments in the global and domestic economy and stands ready to take further action as necessary.

### 4. USD Liquidity Guidance for Commercial Banks

The Central Bank of Kenya draws the attention of all licensed commercial banks to the following guidance regarding foreign exchange operations:

1. **Market-Determined Rates**: All commercial banks are reminded that foreign exchange rates must be determined by market forces. Banks shall quote competitive rates reflecting interbank market conditions and shall not engage in collusive pricing arrangements.

2. **Customer Order Fulfillment**: Banks are expected to fulfill bona fide customer foreign exchange orders within **two (2) business days** of the request, subject to normal KYC/AML verification. Delays in processing legitimate trade-related FX demand will attract supervisory scrutiny.

3. **Position Limits**: Commercial banks shall maintain their overall open foreign currency positions within the prudential limit of **10 percent of core capital**. Any breaches must be reported to the Banking Supervision Department within 24 hours.

4. **Interbank Market Participation**: Banks are encouraged to actively participate in the interbank foreign exchange market to promote price discovery and market efficiency. The CBK will continue to provide USD liquidity through its regular market operations where necessary to smoothen excessive volatility.

5. **Transparency**: All banks must display their prevailing foreign exchange buying and selling rates in a prominent location and update rates at least **twice daily** during business hours.

### 6. FX Market Intervention Stance

The Central Bank of Kenya reiterates its policy of intervening in the foreign exchange market only to smoothen excessive volatility and disorderly market conditions. The CBK does not target a specific exchange rate level.

**Key observations on the FX market (May 2026):**

- The Kenya Shilling traded in the range of **KES 128.50 – KES 132.80** per US Dollar during May 2026.
- The weighted average interbank exchange rate depreciated by approximately **1.2 percent** month-on-month, reflecting seasonal demand for USD related to oil imports and external debt service.
- Diaspora remittance inflows remained resilient at **USD 362 million** in May 2026 (up 4.1% year-on-year), providing a partial offset to outward FX demand.
- Horticulture and tea export receipts continued to provide a stable source of USD supply.

The CBK conducted **USD 120 million** in market support operations during May 2026 through its regular auction mechanism, aimed at addressing temporary supply-demand mismatches. These interventions were consistent with the Bank's stated objective of preventing excessive exchange rate volatility.

Commercial banks are advised to plan their USD sourcing activities prudently and to maintain adequate foreign currency buffers to meet customer demand.

---

### 7. Outlook

The MPC's assessment is that the current monetary policy stance is appropriate and consistent with the inflation target. Key risks to the outlook include:

- **External**: Tightening of global financial conditions, fluctuations in crude oil prices, and geopolitical uncertainties affecting supply chains.
- **Domestic**: Potential fiscal pressures from development expenditure and the pace of credit growth recovery.
- **FX Market**: Seasonal bunching of import demand in Q3 may exert temporary depreciation pressure on the shilling.

The next MPC meeting is scheduled for **July 30, 2026**.
